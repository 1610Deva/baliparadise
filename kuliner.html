Food of a Million Flavors

Balinese food presents a variety of flavors that are comfortable in the mouth. The combination of various spices and herbs that are abundant in each dish makes Balinese cuisine one of the iconic culinary in Indonesia.

Sweet and savory at its best

Some typical Balinese desserts, present savory and sweet flavors. This seems to be commonplace in culinary circles. However, typical Balinese desserts offer their own uniqueness in terms of presentation, taste, and also the deliciousness that tempts your appetite to try it.

Balinese Iconic Main Course

Balinese iconic main courses like "Babi Guling" (roasted suckling pig) and "Ayam Betutu" (slow-cooked spiced chicken) are renowned for their rich, aromatic flavors and traditional spices. These dishes reflect the island’s vibrant culinary heritage, blending unique Indonesian ingredients and techniques.

Balinese Favorite Drinks

Es Daluman

Es Daluman is a refreshing Balinese dessert drink made with daluman (green grass jelly), coconut milk, and sweet palm sugar syrup. It's a popular choice to cool down in Bali’s tropical heat, offering a balance of creamy and sweet flavors.

Tuak

Tuak is a traditional Balinese alcoholic drink made from fermented palm sap, known for its sweet and mildly tangy flavor. Often consumed during social gatherings and ceremonies, it’s a local favorite with varying alcohol content depending on fermentation.

Loloh

Loloh is a traditional Balinese herbal drink made from a blend of leaves, herbs, and spices, known for its health benefits. It’s typically consumed for its refreshing taste and natural healing properties, ranging from boosting immunity to aiding digestion.
